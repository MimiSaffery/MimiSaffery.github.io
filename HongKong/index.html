HONG KONG

Known for its vibrant city and beautiful city skylines,

Hong Kong has some unmissable photo opportunities

1/12

'Choi Hung' meaning rainbow has become an instagram famous spot. Since winning the 1965 HKIA Annual Award, it has been the backdrop to many award winning photographs. Regardless, amateurs and professionals alike have flocked to Choi Hung Estate to caputure the beauty of this colourful apartment block.

2/12

Sky100 is an indoor observational deck offering a 360 degree view of the famous Hong Kong skyline. Located on the 100th floor of the International Commerce Centre, it is sure to give you a great view. Asides from the amazing view, Sky100 also offers an eatery, appropriately named Caf√© 100 as well as avirtual reality attraction, augmented reality photo-taking, and a photo booth.

3/12

At 552m, Victoria Peak is the highest hill on Hong Kong Island. With options to hike, take a taxi or to hop on the Peak Tram, the latter is the most enjoyable way to reach The Peak. Its panoramic view attracts over 7 million visitors each year with views of the city and the waterfront. The viewing deck, Sky Terrace 428 allows visitors to utilise coin operated telescopes for an even better view. This view is one that you should definitely not miss.

4/12

An insight into old Hong Kong architecture, Montane Mansion offers a beautiful backdrop to photos due to its towering buildings. It is a popular spot from photography enthusiasts!

5/12

Grahan Street Market is the original Hong Kong style 'wet market' which continues to be an operating street market. Besides from being an outdoor market, it is also known for its unique grafitti which has provided a great backdrop to many instagram photos.

6/12

Located on Lantau Island, one of the largest islands in Hong Kong, Big Buddha is the world's largest outdoor seated bronze Buddha statue. It is measuyre to be more than 34 metres high and can be seen from a great distance. Six smaller bronze statues known as the Offering of the six Devas surround the Buddha symbolising the six perfections of generosity, morality, patience, zeal, meditation and wisdom. All six of which are necessary for enlightenment. One of the most popular and more scenic ways to access the Buddha is by the Ngong Ping 360 gondola lift.

7/12

A short 10 minute walk from the Tian Tan Buddha, the Wisdom Path is a monument consisting of 38 wooden columns in which verses from the centuries-old Heart Sutra have been carved.

8/12

Madrid based street artist, Okuda San Miguel transformed this ordinary building on Tai Nan Street with this mural that he named, Rainbow Thief. It is now one of the most recognisable and instagrammable building known in Hong Kong.

9/12

The Art Park and waterfront promenade is open to the public offering stunning views of Vicotria Harbour and Hong Kong Island. It is the green heart of the cultural district offering open green space for leisure and relaxation. The Art Park provides a vibrant venue for open air performances, art exhibitions and various cultural events throughout the year.

10/12

Approximately 8.5km, Dragon's Back is an unmissable trail that offers a unique view of the contrasts what Hong Kong is known for, the vibrant city. The Sweeping views over the South China Sea shows a more serene version of Hong Kong with it's lush green trees and fresh ocean air. Often proclaimed as one of the most beautifyl urban walking trails in the world, this is not one to miss.

11/12

Chi Lin Nunnery was founded in 1934 as a retreat for Buddhist nuns. In 1998 it was rebuilt following traditional Tang Dynasty architecture. The temple halls contains statues of the Skyamuni Buddha, the goddess of mercy Guanyin and barious other bodhisattvas. A bodhisattva is any person known to be on the path towards Buddhahood. Today, the temple halls and Chinese Garden in front of the nursery are both open to the public to enjoy its serene grounds.

12/12

A Symphony of Lights is a the world's largest permanent light and sound shows according to Guinness World Records. It is an orchestration of musics, decoration lights, laser light displat and pyrotechnic fireworks. 42 participating buildings on both sides of Victoria Harbour contribute to the light show with various types of lighting effects. The Symphony of Lights is a popular daily event attended by families, and photographers alike.
